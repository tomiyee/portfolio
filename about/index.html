About Me

(First of all, I exist.)

Passions

In short, I have three core passions: software development, machine learning, and education.

1. Software Development

This should go without saying, but I enjoy building software. I had grown up loving to build and assumble furniture from Ikea with my sisters, folding origami, and making homemade ice-cream.

Generally, I just loved putting something together, looking at the final product, and being able to say I made that.

However, growing up with limited resources, I wasn't really able to explore building something "useful" in the physical world aside from the rare piece of Ikea furniture. Thus, I looked to software development which was free for anyone with a computer to explore and learn if they're willing to do some googling.

2. Machine Learning

In the summer of 2017, before my Senior year of high-school, I took part in an internship program at Stanford through their RISE program, where I developed a time-series forecasting LSTM Neural Network for the Stanford Autonomous Systems Laboratory.

This was my first time using Github, Python, Tensorflow, and Keras. I was provided with links to online resources for the introductory machine learning classes and self-studied some basic background in linear algebra and beginning concepts like back-propogation and mean squared erorr. I was so inspired by my readings that I continued to learn how to develop AI models of my very own.

As of Spring 2020, I have completed MIT's Introduction to Machine Learning class (6.036), and have completed four semesters of undergraduate research under the MIT Computer Science and Artifial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL).

3. Education

One of my core values is to do anything that I can to help others. For me, this makes the field of education especially rewarding. Many of my early volunteer work and internships involved leading after-school or summer programs.

Even recently, I've done undergraduate research and software development for the visual coding platform MIT App Inventor during the semester at CSAIL, as well as lead presentations and classes teaching programming to children and adults alike.

Background

I have experience in a variety of programming environments, but I began with developing webpages when I was in middle school, albeit not visually appealing websites. As a result, my "mother tongue" of coding languages is Javascript, with HTML and CSS. I am very familiar with other programming languages like Python, C/C++, and Java, in order of decreasing familiarity.

My first large project was a website that allowed other middle schoolers to practice their arithmetic online, which in hindsight closely resembled Khan Academy (in goal, no where near in visuals or functionality). It simply generated a set of random problems of varying difficulty, like a set of simple multiplication problems, or simple equations to solve for x.
